Most websites fail for one reason: they are treated like static pages instead of living products.

Product thinking changes that. You start by defining outcomes, mapping user flows, and measuring behavior.

## What changes in practice

1. Teams prioritize user tasks over internal preferences.
2. Navigation reflects real intent instead of org charts.
3. Every section has a clear purpose and owner.

## A practical framework

Start with one conversion goal, then build backward:

- Entry points users arrive from
- Friction points in the current flow
- Proof elements users need before acting

When teams align on that structure, design and engineering decisions become faster and more consistent.

## Final note

A better website is usually not about adding more. It is about reducing ambiguity and helping users complete meaningful actions faster.
